# Counterparty Due Diligence Skill

You conduct background research and reputation analysis on individuals and entities to identify risks, verify claims, and support informed decision-making.

**Important**: This provides a research framework, not investigative services. For high-stakes transactions, engage professional due diligence firms. Always comply with applicable privacy laws and regulations.

## Due Diligence Framework

### Levels of Due Diligence

| Level | When to Use | Depth | Typical Cost |
|-------|-------------|-------|--------------|
| **Basic** | Low-value vendors, contractors | Public records, basic search | DIY / minimal |
| **Standard** | Partners, key hires, mid-value deals | + References, media, litigation | $500-2,000 |
| **Enhanced** | Major partners, executives, large deals | + Deep background, financials | $2,000-10,000 |
| **Comprehensive** | M&A, C-suite, high-risk situations | + On-ground investigation | $10,000-50,000+ |

### Due Diligence Categories

**Category 1: Identity & Background**
- Full legal name and aliases
- Date of birth, citizenship
- Current and prior addresses
- Education verification
- Employment history verification

**Category 2: Professional History**
- Business ownership history
- Board positions and affiliations
- Professional licenses
- Industry reputation
- Career progression

**Category 3: Legal & Compliance**
- Civil litigation history
- Criminal record check
- Bankruptcy filings
- Regulatory actions
- Sanctions/watchlist screening

**Category 4: Financial**
- Credit history (with consent)
- Asset verification
- Liens and judgments
- Business financials
- Source of funds/wealth

**Category 5: Reputation**
- Media coverage
- Social media presence
- Industry references
- Professional network analysis
- Controversy/scandal history

## Research Methodology

### Public Records Search

**Individual Records**:
| Source | Information | Access |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Court records (PACER, state) | Federal and state litigation | Often free or low cost |
| Secretary of State | Business registrations, officer roles | Usually free online |
| Property records | Real estate ownership, liens | County recorder websites |
| UCC filings | Secured transactions | State database |
| Bankruptcy (PACER) | Bankruptcy filings | $0.10/page |
| Professional licenses | License status, disciplinary actions | State licensing boards |

**Corporate Records**:
| Source | Information | Access |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| SEC EDGAR | Public company filings | Free |
| State corporate database | Formation, officers, status | Varies by state |
| OFAC/sanctions lists | Sanctions, restricted parties | Free |
| Patent/trademark database | IP ownership | Free (USPTO) |

### Media & Reputation Research

**Search Strategy**:
```
1. Primary search: "Full Name" + company/role
2. Negative search: "Full Name" + lawsuit/fraud/scandal/controversy
3. Industry search: "Full Name" + [industry keywords]
4. Historical search: Filter by date ranges for past periods
5. Social media: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram
6. Industry publications: Trade press, conference appearances
```

**Platforms to Check**:
- Google News (current and archived)
- LinkedIn profile and activity
- Twitter/X presence and statements
- Crunchbase (for tech/startup)
- Glassdoor (employer reputation)
- BBB (business complaints)
- Industry-specific databases

### Reference Verification

**Reference Types**:
| Type | Value | Reliability |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Provided references | Low-medium | Subject selected them |
| Back-channel references | High | Independent verification |
| Former colleagues | Medium-high | Direct observation |
| Former investors/partners | High | Financial relationship |
| Industry peers | Medium | Competitive dynamics |

**Reference Questions**:
```
Professional Competence:
- How do you know [Name]? How long?
- What role did they have? What were their responsibilities?
- What were their key strengths?
- What were their development areas?
- How did they handle [specific challenge]?

Integrity/Character:
- Would you work with them again? Why/why not?
- How did they handle disagreements or conflict?
- Were they honest and transparent in dealings?
- Any concerns about their judgment or ethics?

Deal-Specific:
- Have you been in a partnership/investment with them?
- How did they handle the relationship when things went wrong?
- Did they honor commitments?
- Any surprises or issues post-agreement?
```

### Litigation Search

**Federal Courts (PACER)**:
1. Search by name (individual or entity)
2. Check all relevant districts
3. Review case dockets for details
4. Obtain key filings if relevant

**State Courts**:
1. Identify relevant states (residence, business location)
2. Search each state's court database
3. Both civil and criminal divisions
4. Include variations of name

**What to Look For**:
| Case Type | Red Flag Level | Context |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Personal injury (defendant) | Low | Common, often settled |
| Contract dispute (defendant) | Medium | Pattern matters |
| Fraud allegations | High | Even if dismissed |
| Employment claims (multiple) | Medium-High | Pattern indicates issue |
| Criminal charges | Varies | Depends on nature |
| SEC/regulatory action | High | Securities/compliance |
| Bankruptcy (personal) | Medium | Financial judgment |
| Bankruptcy (business) | Low-Medium | Business failure common |

### Sanctions & Watchlist Screening

**Key Lists to Check**:
| List | Issuer | Risk |
|------|--------|------|
| OFAC SDN | US Treasury | Sanctions violation |
| OFAC Consolidated | US Treasury | Comprehensive sanctions |
| BIS Entity List | Commerce Dept | Export controls |
| FBI Most Wanted | FBI | Criminal |
| Interpol | Interpol | International criminal |
| EU Sanctions | European Union | EU sanctions |
| UN Sanctions | United Nations | International |
| PEP Lists | Various | Politically exposed persons |

## Red Flag Indicators

### High-Risk Red Flags (Immediate Concern)

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Active fraud litigation | Direct integrity concern |
| Sanctions list match | Legal compliance risk |
| Criminal conviction (relevant) | Character/risk concern |
| SEC enforcement action | Securities compliance |
| Pattern of lawsuits | Difficulty working with others |
| Undisclosed bankruptcy | Failed to disclose material fact |
| Inconsistent resume/claims | Dishonesty indicator |
| Negative references (multiple) | Reputation confirmed |

### Medium-Risk Red Flags (Investigate Further)

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Single lawsuit (context-dependent) | May be normal business |
| Business failure history | Execution risk, but common |
| Short tenure at companies | Potential performance issues |
| Gaps in employment | May have explanation |
| Social media controversy | Judgment concern |
| Negative press (single) | May be one-sided story |
| Reference reluctant to speak | May indicate concerns |

### Contextual Factors

| Factor | Considerations |
|--------|---------------|
| Industry norms | Some industries more litigious |
| Role/seniority | Senior people attract more scrutiny |
| Time passed | Old issues less relevant if reformed |
| Pattern vs. isolated | One-off vs. repeated behavior |
| Disclosure | Did they disclose proactively? |
| Explanation | Is there a reasonable explanation? |

## Counterparty Risk Assessment

### Risk Scoring Framework

**Score each category 1-5**:

```

**Subject**: [Name/Entity]
**Assessment Date**: [Date]
**Assessor**: [Name]

### Category Scores

| Category | Score (1-5) | Weight | Weighted |
|----------|-------------|--------|----------|
| Verified identity/background | [X] | 15% | [X] |
| Professional track record | [X] | 20% | [X] |
| Legal/litigation history | [X] | 20% | [X] |
| Financial standing | [X] | 15% | [X] |
| Reputation/references | [X] | 20% | [X] |
| Disclosure/transparency | [X] | 10% | [X] |
| **Overall Score** | | | [X/5.0] |

### Risk Level

- 4.0-5.0: Low Risk — Proceed with standard terms
- 3.0-3.9: Moderate Risk — Proceed with enhanced protections
- 2.0-2.9: Elevated Risk — Significant concerns, reconsider or strong protections
- 1.0-1.9: High Risk — Recommend against proceeding
```

### Mitigation Strategies by Risk Level

**Elevated Risk (proceed with protections)**:
- Enhanced contractual protections
- Escrow arrangements
- Personal guarantees
- More frequent reporting/oversight
- Tighter milestones and checkpoints
- Stronger termination rights

**High Risk (if proceeding anyway)**:
- Collateral/security
- Third-party guarantees
- Performance bonds
- Limited scope engagement first
- Enhanced monitoring
- Exit provisions

## Limitations

**This skill does NOT:**
- Replace professional investigative services
- Access non-public databases
- Conduct interviews or field investigation
- Provide legal conclusions
- Guarantee completeness of findings

**Always recommend:**
- Professional due diligence firms for high-stakes deals
- Legal counsel for interpreting findings
- Compliance with privacy laws (FCRA, GDPR, etc.)
- Subject consent where required
- Multiple source verification
