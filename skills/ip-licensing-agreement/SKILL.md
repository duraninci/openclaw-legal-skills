# IP & Domain Licensing Agreement Skill

You analyze intellectual property and domain licensing structures, helping evaluate terms, identify risks, and optimize deal structures.

**Important**: This is analytical assistance, not legal advice. IP licensing involves complex property rights. All agreements should be reviewed by qualified IP attorneys.

## License vs. Assignment

### Key Differences

| Aspect | License | Assignment |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Ownership | Licensor retains ownership | Full ownership transfers |
| Duration | Can be limited term | Permanent |
| Reversibility | Can be revocable or terminable | Generally irreversible |
| Control | Licensor maintains some control | Complete loss of control |
| Future rights | Licensor can license to others (if non-exclusive) | No rights retained |
| Tax treatment | Royalty income | Capital gains potential |
| Bankruptcy | May be rejected in bankruptcy | Transfer complete |

### When to Use Each

**Use License When:**
- Want to retain ownership
- Need ongoing royalty stream
- Want reversionary rights
- Multiple licensees desired (non-exclusive)
- Temporary arrangement
- Control over use is important

**Use Assignment When:**
- Clean transfer desired
- One-time payment preferred
- No ongoing relationship wanted
- Buyer requires full ownership
- M&A transaction

## License Types

### Exclusivity

| Type | Licensor Can Also Use? | Licensor Can License Others? |
|------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Exclusive** | ❌ No | ❌ No |
| **Sole** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Non-Exclusive** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |

**Exclusive License:**
- Only licensee can use the IP
- Licensor cannot use or license to others
- Highest value to licensee
- Highest risk to licensor

**Sole License:**
- Licensee and licensor can both use
- Licensor cannot license to others
- Middle ground

**Non-Exclusive License:**
- Licensee gets usage rights
- Licensor can license to unlimited others
- Lowest value to licensee
- Lowest risk to licensor

### Scope Limitations

| Dimension | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| **Field of Use** | Specific industry, application, or use case |
| **Territory** | Geographic limitations (country, region, worldwide) |
| **Term** | Fixed period, perpetual, renewable |
| **Channels** | Online, retail, wholesale, specific platforms |
| **Volume** | Quantity limits, user limits |

## Domain Name Licensing

### Domain License Structure

**Key Terms:**

| Term | Description | Standard |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Grant** | Rights being licensed | Exclusive use of domain for specified purposes |
| **Exclusivity** | Exclusive vs. non-exclusive | Usually exclusive for primary domain |
| **Territory** | Geographic scope | Usually worldwide for domains |
| **Term** | Duration | 1-5 years typical, or perpetual |
| **Renewal** | Auto-renewal or manual | Specify clearly |
| **Sublicense** | Can licensee sublicense? | Usually restricted |
| **Modifications** | Can licensee modify/point domain? | Usually yes with restrictions |

### Domain License vs. Domain Purchase

| Factor | License | Purchase |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Upfront cost | Lower | Higher |
| Ongoing cost | Royalties/fees | None (except registration) |
| Ownership | Remains with licensor | Transfers |
| Risk of loss | Can be terminated | Permanent ownership |
| Control | Licensor retains some | Full control |
| Exit value | Must renegotiate | Asset to sell |

### Domain License Provisions

**Essential Provisions:**

```
1. GRANT OF LICENSE
Licensor grants to Licensee an exclusive, [irrevocable/revocable], 
worldwide license to use the Domain Name [domain.com] for the purpose 
of [specified business purposes].

2. TERM
This License shall commence on [Date] and continue for [X] years,
unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Agreement.
[OR: This License shall be perpetual.]

3. LICENSE FEE
Licensee shall pay Licensor:
(a) An initial license fee of $[X]; and
(b) [Annual royalty of $X / X% of revenue / No ongoing fee]

4. EXCLUSIVITY
During the Term, Licensor shall not license the Domain Name to any 
third party or use the Domain Name for any competing purpose.

5. DOMAIN CONTROL
(a) Licensor shall maintain registration of the Domain Name
(b) Licensee shall have authority to configure DNS settings
(c) Licensor shall not transfer or encumber the Domain Name

6. IRREVOCABILITY [if applicable]
This License is irrevocable and may not be terminated by Licensor 
except for material breach by Licensee that remains uncured for 
[30] days after written notice.

7. SUBLICENSING
Licensee [may/may not] sublicense the Domain Name without 
Licensor's prior written consent.

8. REPRESENTATIONS
Licensor represents and warrants that:
(a) Licensor is the sole owner of the Domain Name
(b) The Domain Name is free of liens and encumbrances
(c) Licensor has full authority to grant this License
```

### Irrevocability

**What "Irrevocable" Means:**
- Licensor cannot terminate at will
- Only terminable for specific causes (material breach)
- Survives licensor bankruptcy (with limitations)
- Survives change of ownership of the IP

**Limitations on Irrevocability:**
- Licensee breach can still trigger termination
- Certain regulatory events may override
- Bankruptcy trustee may have rights (see below)

**Bankruptcy Considerations:**
- Under 11 USC 365(n), IP licensee has election rights
- Can retain rights if IP is "intellectual property" under code
- Domain names may not be clearly covered
- Consider adding explicit bankruptcy provisions

## Royalty Structures

### Common Royalty Models

| Model | Structure | Best For |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Fixed Fee** | $X per year | Predictable, simple |
| **Revenue Share** | X% of gross revenue | Aligns with success |
| **Per-Unit** | $X per sale/user | Volume-based |
| **Hybrid** | Minimum + percentage | Base + upside |
| **Milestone** | Payments at milestones | Development stage |

### Royalty Rate Benchmarks

| IP Type | Typical Range |
|---------|---------------|
| Patents | 2-7% of revenue |
| Trademarks | 1-5% of revenue |
| Software | 10-25% of revenue |
| Domain names | $1K-$100K+/year or 1-5% |
| Content/Media | 5-15% of revenue |

### Royalty Provisions

```
ROYALTY PAYMENTS

1. Calculation
Licensee shall pay Licensor a royalty equal to [X]% of Qualified 
Revenue, calculated [monthly/quarterly/annually].

2. Definition of Qualified Revenue
"Qualified Revenue" means [gross revenue / net revenue / specific 
revenue streams] directly attributable to [the licensed IP / products 
using the licensed IP / the licensed domain].

3. Minimum Royalty
Notwithstanding the above, Licensee shall pay a minimum annual 
royalty of $[X], regardless of actual Qualified Revenue.

4. Reporting
Licensee shall provide Licensor with [monthly/quarterly] reports 
showing calculation of Qualified Revenue and royalties due.

5. Audit Rights
Licensor shall have the right to audit Licensee's books and records 
related to Qualified Revenue upon [30] days' notice, no more than 
[once per year].

6. Payment Terms
Royalties are due within [30] days of the end of each [month/quarter].
Late payments shall accrue interest at [X]% per annum.
```

## Key Provisions Analysis

### Grant Clause

**What to Check:**
- [ ] Is the grant clearly stated?
- [ ] Is the IP clearly identified?
- [ ] Is the scope of use defined?
- [ ] Are exclusivity terms clear?
- [ ] Territory defined?
- [ ] Field of use limitations?

**Red Flags:**
- Vague description of licensed IP
- "All intellectual property" without specificity
- Unclear exclusivity terms
- Missing territory definition

### Term and Termination

**What to Check:**
- [ ] Is the term clearly stated?
- [ ] Renewal terms (auto vs. manual)?
- [ ] Termination for convenience allowed?
- [ ] Termination for cause triggers?
- [ ] Notice periods?
- [ ] Post-termination rights?

**Termination Triggers (Typical):**
| Trigger | Notice Period | Cure Period |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Material breach | 30 days | 30 days |
| Non-payment | 15 days | 15 days |
| Bankruptcy | Immediate | None |
| Change of control | 30-90 days | None |
| Convenience | 90-180 days | N/A |

### Sublicensing

**Options:**
| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Prohibited** | No sublicensing allowed |
| **Consent Required** | Sublicense only with licensor approval |
| **Notice Required** | Sublicense with notice, no approval |
| **Unlimited** | Free to sublicense |

**Sublicense Considerations:**
- Does sublicense survive termination of main license?
- Does licensee remain liable for sublicensee's actions?
- Are sublicense terms required to match main license?
- Does licensor have approval over sublicensee identity?

### Representations and Warranties

**Licensor Should Warrant:**
- [ ] Ownership of the IP
- [ ] Authority to license
- [ ] No infringement of third-party rights
- [ ] No liens or encumbrances
- [ ] IP is valid (for patents/trademarks)

**Licensee Should Warrant:**
- [ ] Authority to enter agreement
- [ ] Will use IP only as permitted
- [ ] Will comply with applicable laws
- [ ] Will maintain quality standards (if required)

### Indemnification

**Typical Structure:**
- Licensor indemnifies for IP infringement claims
- Licensee indemnifies for misuse of IP
- Cross-indemnity for breaches
- Caps on indemnification (often tied to fees paid)

## Domain License Specific Issues

### DNS Control

**Options:**
| Model | Who Controls DNS? | Risk Level |
|-------|-------------------|------------|
| **Full Control to Licensee** | Licensee | Low for licensee, high for licensor |
| **Shared Control** | Both parties | Medium |
| **Licensor Control** | Licensor | High for licensee, low for licensor |

**Best Practice:** Licensee controls DNS for operation, but cannot transfer registration.

### Registration Maintenance

**Key Terms:**
- Who pays registration fees?
- Who is responsible for renewal?
- What happens if registration lapses?
- Notice requirements if registration at risk

### Domain Transfer Restrictions

**Standard Provision:**
```
TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

Licensor shall not sell, transfer, assign, or encumber the Domain Name 
during the Term without Licensee's prior written consent, which may be 
withheld in Licensee's sole discretion.

Any purported transfer in violation of this provision shall be void 
and shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement.
```

### Right of First Refusal on Sale

**If licensor wants to sell the domain:**
```
RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

If Licensor receives a bona fide offer to purchase the Domain Name that 
Licensor wishes to accept, Licensor shall first offer to sell the Domain 
Name to Licensee on the same terms. Licensee shall have [30] days to 
accept such offer. If Licensee declines or fails to respond, Licensor 
may complete the sale to the third party on terms no more favorable 
than those offered to Licensee.
```

## Checklist for Domain/IP License Review

### Licensor Perspective
- [ ] Retained ownership clearly stated
- [ ] Royalty/fee structure adequate
- [ ] Audit rights included
- [ ] Quality control provisions (for trademark)
- [ ] Termination rights preserved
- [ ] Sublicensing restricted
- [ ] Post-termination transition plan

### Licensee Perspective
- [ ] Exclusivity clearly granted
- [ ] Term sufficient for business needs
- [ ] Irrevocability (if needed)
- [ ] Sublicensing rights (if needed)
- [ ] DNS/operational control
- [ ] Protections against licensor sale
- [ ] ROFR on domain purchase
- [ ] Bankruptcy protection
