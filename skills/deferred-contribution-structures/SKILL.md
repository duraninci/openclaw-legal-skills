# Deferred Contribution Structures Skill

You analyze arrangements where parties agree to partnership/LLC terms but delay actual asset contributions, helping structure interim arrangements and contribution triggers.

**Important**: Deferred contribution structures involve complex legal, tax, and business considerations. All structures should be reviewed by qualified attorneys and tax advisors.

## Overview

### What is a Deferred Contribution Structure?

An arrangement where:
1. Parties agree to partnership/LLC terms (binding agreement)
2. BUT actual asset contributions are delayed
3. Interim arrangements govern the relationship
4. Contributions occur at predetermined triggers

### Why Use Deferred Contributions?

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Liability protection** | Assets stay in original owner's name; protected from other party's creditors |
| **Bank/loan issues** | Avoid need for multiple signatures on financing |
| **Tax timing** | Optimize for QSBS, capital gains, or other tax benefits |
| **Relationship testing** | Prove partnership works before full commitment |
| **Flexibility** | Easier to unwind if things don't work out |
| **Valuation uncertainty** | Lock in terms, contribute when values are clearer |

### Risks of Deferred Contributions

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Enforcement** | Harder to enforce contribution if party refuses |
| **Creditor issues** | Bankruptcy trustee may reject executory contract |
| **Tax complexity** | Interim profit sharing may have adverse tax treatment |
| **Valuation changes** | Asset values may shift before contribution |
| **Control gaps** | Who controls assets during interim period? |

## Structure Components

### 1. Binding Operating Agreement

Even without contribution, parties can have binding agreement on:
- Ownership percentages (upon contribution)
- Profit/loss allocation
- Governance and decision rights
- Exit and sale terms
- Contribution timing/triggers

```
BINDING COMMITMENT

The parties agree that this Operating Agreement shall be binding 
upon execution, notwithstanding that the Initial Contributions 
described in Section [X] shall occur at the Contribution Date 
rather than upon execution.

Each party hereby irrevocably commits to make its Initial 
Contribution upon the occurrence of the Contribution Trigger.
```

### 2. Contribution Trigger Events

**Common Triggers:**

| Trigger | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Fixed Date** | Contributions due on specific date (e.g., 2 years from signing) |
| **Mutual Agreement** | Contributions when both parties agree |
| **Funding Event** | Upon raising $X in capital |
| **Revenue Milestone** | Upon reaching $X revenue |
| **Acquisition Offer** | If offer received above $X |
| **QSBS Optimization** | When tax advisor recommends |
| **External Event** | Change of law, market condition |

```
CONTRIBUTION TRIGGER

The Initial Contributions shall be made within thirty (30) days 
following the earliest of:
(a) The second (2nd) anniversary of the Effective Date;
(b) The closing of a Qualified Financing of at least $[X];
(c) Receipt of a bona fide acquisition offer of at least $[X];
(d) Written agreement of both Members to accelerate; or
(e) Establishment of the QSBS Structure as determined by 
    the Company's tax advisors.
```

### 3. Interim Period Arrangements

During the period before contribution:

**Option A: License Model**
- Asset owner licenses asset to operating business
- Exclusive, irrevocable license
- Operating party builds business using licensed asset
- No formal ownership transfer

**Option B: Profits Interest Model**
- One party receives profits interest (not capital interest)
- Participates in profits without contributing capital
- Contribution converts to capital interest later

**Option C: Management Agreement Model**
- One party manages assets owned by other
- Fees or profit share as compensation
- No ownership transfer until trigger

**Option D: Joint Venture Model**
- Separate entity for operations
- Each party contributes services/resources (not assets)
- Assets remain separately owned until trigger

### 4. Interim Profit Sharing

**Structure Options:**

| Structure | Tax Treatment | Complexity |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Guaranteed Payment** | Ordinary income to recipient; deduction to payer | Low |
| **Profits Interest** | Partnership income allocation | Medium |
| **License Royalty** | Royalty income to licensor | Low |
| **Management Fee** | Service income | Low |
| **Distribution** | Depends on entity structure | Varies |

**Tax Considerations:**
- Profits interest: No tax on grant if properly structured (Rev. Proc. 93-27)
- Guaranteed payment: Ordinary income, self-employment tax
- Royalty: Ordinary income, possible 199A deduction
- Distribution from LLC: Pass-through character

### 5. Commitment Security

How to ensure parties actually contribute when triggered:

**Mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Specific Performance** | Agreement that contribution can be court-ordered |
| **Liquidated Damages** | Agreed damages if party fails to contribute |
| **Security Interest** | Pledge of asset as security for commitment |
| **Escrow** | Asset held in escrow pending trigger |
| **Power of Attorney** | Irrevocable POA to effect contribution |
| **Personal Guarantee** | Individual guarantees contribution |

```
SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE

The parties acknowledge that the assets to be contributed are unique 
and that damages would be an inadequate remedy for breach of the 
commitment to contribute. Accordingly, each party shall be entitled 
to specific performance of the other party's contribution obligation, 
without the need to prove actual damages or post bond.
```

## Tax Analysis

### Section 721 Timing

**General Rule:** Contributions to partnership are tax-free under Section 721.

**Timing Question:** When does the "contribution" occur?
- At agreement execution? (No, if asset not transferred)
- At trigger event? (Yes, if that's when transfer occurs)
- Gradual? (Possible if structured that way)

**Implication:** Tax-free treatment applies when actual contribution occurs, not when agreement is signed.

### Interim Profit Payments

**If paying profits before contribution:**

| Method | Tax Character |
|--------|---------------|
| **As partner with profits interest** | Ordinary/capital depending on items |
| **As contractor (management fee)** | Ordinary income + SE tax |
| **As licensor (royalty)** | Ordinary income |
| **As employee (salary)** | W-2 wages |

**Best Practice:** Structure as profits interest if possible for tax efficiency.

### 704(c) Issues

**When contribution occurs:**
- Built-in gain/loss at contribution date determines 704(c) allocations
- If asset appreciated during interim period, more built-in gain
- May affect allocations going forward

### QSBS Considerations

**Holding Period:** QSBS 5-year holding period starts at contribution, not at agreement.

**Planning:** May want to contribute early enough to satisfy holding period before anticipated exit.

### Disguised Sale Risk

**IRS may recharacterize as sale if:**
- "Contributing" party receives cash around same time as "contribution"
- Economic substance looks like sale, not contribution
- Debt assumptions involved

**Safe Harbor:** No presumption of disguised sale if contributions and distributions are >2 years apart.

## Operating Agreement Provisions

### Core Provisions for Deferred Structure

```
ARTICLE X: DEFERRED CONTRIBUTIONS

Section 10.1 Commitment to Contribute
Each Member hereby irrevocably commits to contribute the assets 
set forth on Exhibit A (the "Committed Assets") to the Company 
upon the occurrence of a Contribution Trigger.

Section 10.2 Contribution Triggers
A "Contribution Trigger" shall mean the earliest of:
(a) [Fixed date];
(b) [Financing event];
(c) [Acquisition event];
(d) [Mutual agreement];
(e) [Other triggers].

Section 10.3 Contribution Procedure
Upon occurrence of a Contribution Trigger:
(a) The Company shall provide written notice to each Member;
(b) Each Member shall execute all documents necessary to transfer 
    the Committed Assets within [30] days;
(c) Failure to contribute shall constitute a material breach.

Section 10.4 Interim Period
During the period prior to contribution of the Committed Assets:
(a) Each Member shall retain ownership of its Committed Assets;
(b) [Interim arrangements - license/management/etc.];
(c) Profits and losses shall be allocated as set forth in Article [Y];
(d) Neither Member shall encumber, transfer, or impair the 
    Committed Assets without consent.

Section 10.5 Pre-Contribution Obligations
During the Interim Period, each Member shall:
(a) Maintain the Committed Assets in good condition;
(b) Not create any liens or encumbrances;
(c) Maintain adequate insurance;
(d) Provide reasonable access for inspection;
(e) Operate [relevant business] in ordinary course.

Section 10.6 Failure to Contribute
If a Member fails to contribute its Committed Assets after a 
Contribution Trigger:
(a) The other Member may seek specific performance;
(b) The breaching Member shall pay liquidated damages of $[X];
(c) The non-breaching Member may terminate this Agreement; and
(d) [Other remedies].

Section 10.7 Interim Profit Allocation
During the Interim Period, profits and losses shall be allocated:
(a) [X]% to DI Entity;
(b) [Y]% to FS Entity;
notwithstanding that the Initial Contributions have not occurred.

Section 10.8 Representations Regarding Committed Assets
Each Member represents and warrants that:
(a) It owns the Committed Assets free of liens;
(b) It has authority to contribute the Committed Assets;
(c) The Committed Assets are in good condition;
(d) No consent is required for contribution.
```

### License Agreement (For Domain/IP)

```
INTERIM LICENSE AGREEMENT

1. Grant of License
Licensor grants to Licensee an exclusive, irrevocable, worldwide 
license to use the Licensed Asset for the Business during the 
Interim Period.

2. Irrevocability
This License may not be revoked or terminated by Licensor except 
for material breach by Licensee that remains uncured for [30] days.

3. Automatic Termination on Contribution
This License shall automatically terminate upon contribution of 
the Licensed Asset to the Company pursuant to the Operating Agreement.

4. No Charge / Royalty
[No license fee during Interim Period]
OR
[License fee of $X per [month/year] during Interim Period]
OR
[Royalty of X% of Revenue during Interim Period]

5. Sublicensing
Licensee may sublicense to the Company and its affiliates without 
consent.

6. Survival
The licenses and rights granted herein shall survive any bankruptcy 
of Licensor to the fullest extent permitted by law.
```

### Power of Attorney (For Contribution)

```
IRREVOCABLE POWER OF ATTORNEY

[Member] hereby grants to [Company/Other Member] an irrevocable 
power of attorney, coupled with an interest, to:

(a) Execute and deliver all documents necessary to transfer the 
    Committed Assets to the Company upon a Contribution Trigger;
(b) File any UCC statements or other filings necessary to perfect 
    the Company's interest in the Committed Assets;
(c) Take any other action reasonably necessary to effect the 
    contribution contemplated by the Operating Agreement.

This Power of Attorney is irrevocable and shall survive the 
disability or incapacity of the grantor.
```

## Risk Mitigation

### For the Non-Contributing Party (Waiting for Partner's Contribution)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Partner doesn't contribute | Specific performance clause; liquidated damages |
| Partner encumbers asset | Covenant not to encumber; UCC filing |
| Partner becomes insolvent | Contribution escrow; POA |
| Asset deteriorates | Maintenance covenant; insurance requirement |
| Asset value changes | Fixed valuation or formula |

### For the Contributing Party (Hasn't Contributed Yet)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Business fails, forced to contribute | Contribution conditions; material change termination |
| Don't receive expected payments | Guaranteed minimum; enforcement rights |
| Other party misuses asset | License restrictions; audit rights |
| Valuation unfair at contribution | Pre-agreed valuation; adjustment mechanism |

## Checklist

### Pre-Signing

- [ ] Contribution triggers clearly defined
- [ ] Interim arrangements documented (license, management, etc.)
- [ ] Interim profit sharing structured (tax-efficient)
- [ ] Enforcement mechanisms (specific performance, POA)
- [ ] Asset protection provisions
- [ ] Valuation methodology agreed
- [ ] Tax advisor sign-off on structure
- [ ] Exit provisions if contribution doesn't occur

### Interim Period

- [ ] Committed assets maintained
- [ ] No encumbrances created
- [ ] Insurance maintained
- [ ] Interim payments made
- [ ] Records kept
- [ ] Trigger events monitored

### At Contribution

- [ ] Trigger event documented
- [ ] Contribution documents executed
- [ ] Asset transfer completed
- [ ] Operating agreement updated (if needed)
- [ ] Tax filings prepared
- [ ] 83(b) elections filed (if applicable)
