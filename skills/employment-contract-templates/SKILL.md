# Employment Contracts Skill

You draft and review employment-related agreements, ensuring compliance with federal and state employment laws while protecting both employer and employee interests.

**Important**: Employment law varies significantly by jurisdiction. These templates must be reviewed by qualified employment counsel before use.

## Employment Relationship Framework

### Employment Classification

| Classification | Characteristics | Tax Treatment |
|----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **W-2 Employee** | Company controls work methods, provides tools, sets hours | Employer withholds taxes |
| **1099 Contractor** | Controls own methods, provides own tools, sets own schedule | Self-employment taxes |
| **Statutory Employee** | Specific categories (drivers, salespeople) | W-2 but some 1099 treatment |

### Misclassification Risk Factors

**IRS 20-Factor Test Categories**:
1. **Behavioral Control**: Who directs how work is done?
2. **Financial Control**: Who controls business aspects?
3. **Relationship Type**: Written contracts, benefits, permanence

**Red Flags for Misclassification**:
- Contractor works exclusively for one company
- Company provides all tools/equipment
- Company sets specific hours
- Contractor cannot subcontract work
- Ongoing relationship without defined project
- Company provides training

### At-Will Employment

**Default Rule** (most states): Employment can be terminated by either party, at any time, for any reason (or no reason), without notice.

**Exceptions**:
- Public policy (refusing illegal acts, exercising legal rights)
- Implied contract (handbooks, verbal promises)
- Implied covenant of good faith (some states)
- Statutory protections (discrimination, retaliation)

**States with Unique Rules**:
| State | Notable Provisions |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Montana** | Only state requiring "good cause" after probation |
| **California** | Strong employee protections, no non-competes |
| **New York** | At-will but strong whistleblower protections |

### Standard Offer Letter Template

```markdown

[Date]

[Candidate Name]
[Address]

We are pleased to offer you employment with [Company Name] on the following terms:

## Position and Duties
**Title**: [Job Title]
**Department**: [Department]
**Reports To**: [Manager Name/Title]
**Start Date**: [Date]
**Location**: [Office Location / Remote]

Your duties will include [brief description]. This is a [full-time/part-time] position.

## Compensation
**Base Salary**: $[Amount] per [year/hour], paid [frequency]
**Pay Schedule**: [Bi-weekly/Semi-monthly/Monthly]

## Benefits

- Health insurance (effective [date])
- [401(k)/retirement plan]
- [PTO/vacation policy]
- [Other benefits]

## Equity (if applicable)
Subject to board approval, you will be granted [options/RSUs] for [number] shares of common stock, vesting over [4 years] with a [1-year] cliff. This grant is governed by the [Plan Name] and a separate equity agreement.

## At-Will Employment
Your employment is "at-will," meaning either you or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice. This letter does not constitute a contract of employment for any specific duration.

## Contingencies
This offer is contingent upon:
- Satisfactory background check
- Verification of employment eligibility (I-9)
- [Reference checks]
- [Drug screening if applicable]
- Execution of [Confidentiality Agreement/IP Assignment]

## Acceptance
Please indicate your acceptance by signing below and returning by [Date].

We are excited about the possibility of you joining our team!

Sincerely,

[Authorized Signature]
[Name, Title]

---

**ACCEPTED AND AGREED:**

Signature: _______________________  Date: ____________

Printed Name: ____________________
```

### Executive Offer Letter Additions

For executive/senior positions, add:
- Signing bonus (with clawback provisions)
- Relocation assistance
- Severance terms
- Change of control provisions
- Title/reporting structure guarantees
- Board observer/seat rights

## Employment Agreement Framework

### Full Employment Agreement Template

```markdown
# Employment Agreement

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [Date] by and between:

**Company**: [Legal Name], a [State] [corporation/LLC] ("Company")
**Employee**: [Name] ("Employee")

## 1. Employment and Duties

### 1.1 Position
Company employs Employee as [Title], reporting to [Title/Position].

### 1.2 Duties
Employee shall perform duties customary for this position and as assigned by Company, including:
- [List key responsibilities]
- [Additional duties as assigned]

### 1.3 Best Efforts
Employee shall devote full business time and best efforts to Company. Employee may not engage in other employment or business activities without prior written consent, except for:
- Board positions with prior approval
- Passive investments
- Charitable/civic activities that don't interfere

### 1.4 Location
Employee's primary work location is [Address/Remote].

## 2. Term

### 2.1 At-Will Employment
Employment is at-will and may be terminated by either party at any time, with or without cause or notice, subject to any severance provisions below.

### 2.2 Initial Term (if applicable)
[For fixed-term agreements: Initial term of [X] years, renewable by mutual agreement.]

## 3. Compensation

### 3.1 Base Salary
$[Amount] per year, payable [frequency], less applicable withholdings.

### 3.2 Bonus
Employee is eligible for an annual discretionary bonus of up to [X]% of base salary, based on [individual/company performance]. Bonus terms:
- Pro-rated for partial year
- Must be employed on payment date
- Not earned until paid

### 3.3 Commission (if applicable)
[Commission structure and plan reference]

### 3.4 Equity
Subject to board approval:
- [Option/RSU] grant of [X] shares
- Vesting: [4 years, 1-year cliff, monthly thereafter]
- Governed by [Equity Plan Name]
- Separate equity agreement controls

### 3.5 Benefits
Employee is eligible for standard employee benefits per Company policies, including:
- Health/dental/vision insurance
- [401(k) with X% match]
- [PTO policy]
- [Other benefits]

### 3.6 Expenses
Company will reimburse reasonable business expenses per Company policy.

## 4. Confidentiality and IP

### 4.1 Confidential Information
Employee shall maintain confidentiality of all Company confidential information during and after employment. [Reference separate CIIA if applicable]

### 4.2 Inventions Assignment
All inventions, works, and IP created during employment relating to Company business are Company property. [Reference separate PIIA if applicable]

### 4.3 Return of Materials
Upon termination, Employee shall return all Company property and materials.

## 5. Restrictive Covenants

### 5.1 Non-Competition [IF ENFORCEABLE IN JURISDICTION]
During employment and for [12 months] thereafter, Employee shall not:
- Engage in Competitive Activity in the Territory
- Own, manage, or consult for a Competitor

"Competitive Activity": [Define]
"Territory": [Define]
"Competitor": [Define or list]

### 5.2 Non-Solicitation
For [12-24 months] after termination, Employee shall not:
- Solicit or hire Company employees
- Solicit Company customers/clients with whom Employee had contact
- Interfere with Company business relationships

### 5.3 Non-Disparagement
Neither party shall make disparaging statements about the other.

## 6. Termination

### 6.1 Termination by Company for Cause
Company may terminate immediately for Cause:
- Material breach of this Agreement
- Conviction of felony or crime of dishonesty
- Willful misconduct or gross negligence
- Failure to perform duties after written notice
- Violation of Company policies
- [Other defined events]

### 6.2 Termination Without Cause
Company may terminate without Cause upon [X days] written notice.

### 6.3 Termination by Employee
Employee may terminate upon [X days] written notice.

### 6.4 Severance (if terminated without Cause)
If Company terminates without Cause:
- [X months] base salary continuation
- [X months] COBRA premium payment
- [Accelerated vesting provisions]
- Conditioned on signing release agreement

### 6.5 Change of Control
[Double-trigger or single-trigger provisions]
[Accelerated vesting]
[Enhanced severance]

## 7. General Provisions

### 7.1 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by [State] law.

### 7.2 Arbitration
Disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in [Location] under [AAA/JAMS] rules. [Carve-outs for injunctive relief]

### 7.3 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, together with [list exhibits], constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes prior agreements.

### 7.4 Amendments
Modifications must be in writing signed by both parties.

### 7.5 Severability
Invalid provisions shall be modified to minimum extent necessary.

### 7.6 Waiver
Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver.

### 7.7 Assignment
Employee may not assign. Company may assign to successor.

### 7.8 Counterparts
May be executed in counterparts.

---

**COMPANY**:

By: _______________________
Name:
Title:
Date:

**EMPLOYEE**:

Signature: _______________________
Name:
Date:
```

## Independent Contractor Agreement

### Key Provisions

```markdown
# Independent Contractor Agreement

## 1. Services
Contractor will perform: [Detailed description of services/deliverables]

## 2. Term
[Start date] to [End date] OR [Project completion]

## 3. Compensation
- Fixed fee: $[Amount] for [deliverables]
- Hourly rate: $[Amount]/hour, not to exceed [X] hours
- Payment terms: [Net 30, milestone-based, etc.]
- No benefits, withholdings, or reimbursements

## 4. Independent Contractor Status
### 4.1 Not an Employee
Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee. Contractor is responsible for own taxes, insurance, and benefits.

### 4.2 Control
Contractor controls the manner and means of performing services. Company controls only the results.

### 4.3 No Authority
Contractor has no authority to bind Company or represent as employee/agent.

## 5. Tools and Expenses
Contractor provides own tools, equipment, and workspace. [Or specify what Company provides]

## 6. Confidentiality
[Standard confidentiality provisions]

## 7. Intellectual Property
All work product is work-for-hire and Company property. Contractor assigns all rights.

## 8. Indemnification
Contractor indemnifies Company against claims arising from:
- Contractor's performance of services
- Misclassification claims
- Third-party IP infringement

## 9. Insurance
Contractor shall maintain:
- General liability: $[X] minimum
- Professional liability/E&O: $[X] minimum
- Workers' compensation (if applicable)

## 10. Termination
Either party may terminate with [X days] notice. [Immediate termination for breach]
```

## Non-Compete Analysis Framework

### Enforceability by State

| State | Enforceability | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| **California** | ❌ Banned | Only narrow trade secret exceptions |
| **Oklahoma** | ❌ Banned | Limited exceptions |
| **North Dakota** | ❌ Banned | Very limited |
| **Minnesota** | ❌ Banned (2023) | For agreements after July 2023 |
| **Colorado** | ⚠️ Limited | Only for highly compensated workers |
| **Washington** | ⚠️ Limited | Income threshold required |
| **Oregon** | ⚠️ Limited | Income/duration limits |
| **Massachusetts** | ⚠️ Limited | Garden leave required |
| **Illinois** | ⚠️ Limited | Income threshold |
| **New York** | ✅* | *Potential ban pending |
| **Texas** | ✅ | Ancillary to enforceable agreement |
| **Florida** | ✅ | Employer-friendly |
| **Most states** | ✅ | Reasonableness standard |

### Reasonableness Factors

Courts typically evaluate:
1. **Duration**: Usually 6-24 months maximum
2. **Geographic Scope**: Related to actual business territory
3. **Activity Scope**: Limited to actual competitive activities
4. **Legitimate Interest**: Protecting trade secrets, relationships, training investment
5. **Consideration**: Additional compensation for existing employees
6. **Hardship**: Impact on employee's ability to earn a living

### Non-Compete Review Checklist

- [ ] Is this enforceable in the relevant jurisdiction?
- [ ] Is duration reasonable (under 2 years)?
- [ ] Is geographic scope tied to actual business footprint?
- [ ] Is activity scope narrowly tailored?
- [ ] Does employer have legitimate protectable interest?
- [ ] Is there adequate consideration (for existing employees)?
- [ ] Are there carve-outs for general skills/knowledge?
- [ ] What triggers the restriction (any termination or specific circumstances)?

## Severance Agreement Framework

### Standard Severance Components

```markdown
# Separation Agreement and General Release

## 1. Separation Date
Employment ends [Date].

## 2. Severance Benefits
In exchange for this release, Company will provide:
- [X weeks/months] base salary: $[Amount]
- COBRA premium payment for [X months]
- [Accelerated vesting]
- [Outplacement services]
- [Other benefits]

## 3. General Release
Employee releases all claims against Company, including:
- Wrongful termination
- Discrimination (Title VII, ADA, ADEA, state laws)
- Wage and hour claims
- Contract claims
- Tort claims
- [Comprehensive list]

**Excluded from release**:
- Vested benefits
- Workers' compensation
- Unemployment insurance
- Indemnification rights
- Non-waivable rights

## 4. ADEA Compliance (Age 40+)
- Employee has [21/45] days to consider
- Employee may revoke within 7 days of signing
- Employee advised to consult attorney
- [For group layoffs: Disclosure of ages/titles]

## 5. Confidentiality
Employee will keep separation terms confidential, except:
- Legal/tax advisors
- Spouse
- As required by law

## 6. Non-Disparagement
[Mutual non-disparagement provisions]

## 7. Return of Property
Employee will return all Company property by [Date].

## 8. Ongoing Obligations
- Confidentiality obligations survive
- Non-compete/non-solicit (if applicable)
- Cooperation with litigation

## 9. References
Company will provide neutral reference confirming dates and title only.

## 10. No Admission
This agreement is not an admission of wrongdoing.
```

### ADEA Compliance (Older Workers)

For employees 40+, the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act requires:
- **Individual termination**: 21 days to consider
- **Group termination**: 45 days to consider + disclosure chart
- 7-day revocation period after signing
- Written advice to consult attorney
- Consideration beyond what's already owed
- Clear, understandable language

## Equity Compensation in Employment

### Stock Option Basics

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Grant Date** | Date options are awarded |
| **Exercise Price** | Price to purchase shares (usually FMV at grant) |
| **Vesting Schedule** | Timeline for earning the right to exercise |
| **Cliff** | Initial period before any vesting |
| **Expiration** | Options typically expire 10 years from grant |
| **ISO** | Incentive Stock Options (tax-favored, employees only) |
| **NSO** | Non-Qualified Stock Options (more flexible, taxable at exercise) |

### Standard Vesting Schedule

**4-Year Vesting with 1-Year Cliff**:
- 0-12 months: 0% vested
- 12 months: 25% vests (cliff)
- 13-48 months: ~2.08%/month (or 1/48 total per month)
- 48 months: 100% vested

### Acceleration Provisions

**Single Trigger**: Acceleration upon change of control alone
**Double Trigger**: Acceleration requires change of control + termination

### Key Terms to Review

- [ ] Grant size and exercise price
- [ ] Vesting schedule and cliff
- [ ] Acceleration provisions
- [ ] Post-termination exercise period
- [ ] Early exercise rights
- [ ] Repurchase rights on unvested shares
- [ ] Right of first refusal
- [ ] 83(b) election eligibility

## State-Specific Requirements

### California
- No enforceable non-competes
- Final pay due immediately upon termination
- Accrued vacation must be paid out
- Meal and rest break requirements
- Expense reimbursement required
- Arbitration agreements under scrutiny

### New York
- Non-competes currently enforceable (may change)
- NYC salary transparency required
- Final pay within regular pay cycle
- Wage theft protections
- Sexual harassment training required

### Texas
- Non-competes enforceable if ancillary to agreement
- At-will doctrine strong
- Final pay within 6 days
- Minimal meal/break requirements

## Limitations

**This skill does NOT:**
- Provide legal advice
- Replace employment counsel
- Guarantee enforceability
- Cover all jurisdictions
- Address union/CBA issues
- Cover immigration (visa sponsorship)

**Always recommend:**
- Jurisdiction-specific legal review
- HR policy alignment
- Regular updates for law changes
- Employee handbook consistency
